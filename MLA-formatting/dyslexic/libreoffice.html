How to format LibreOffice for MLA

This guide assumes you have already completed the Printer and Page setup.

  1. Click into the Header, then use the blue dropdown to insert a page number.
  2. Add your last name in front of the page number. Make sure the font is Times New Roman, the size is 12pt, and the alignment is right.
  3. Click out of the Header into the regular body of the page. Set the Line Spacing to "Double". (Make sure the font is Times New Roman, the size is 12pt, and the alignment is left!)
  4. Type your full name on one line, the title and last name of your teacher on the second, the name of the class on the third, and the date the assignment is due on the fourth in "Day Month Year" format.
  5. Center-align the next line, and type the title of your paper, or a placeholder to replace with your eventual title. Make sure to follow the MLA guidelines for capitalization.
  6. Left-align the line after the title, then go to Format --> Paragraph, and change "First Line" to 0.50.
  7. This is your body paragraph. If you press "ENTER", the next line should also be indented!

Works Cited page:

  1. At the end of the document, press "CTRL + ENTER" at the same time to insert a page break. Then center-align the first line of the page you've created and type "Works Cited".
  2. In the next line, go to Format --> Paragraph again. Change "Before text" and "After text" to 0.50, and "First line" to -0.50. (Yes, really.)
  3. Your Works Cited page should now be ready to add your sources to!
